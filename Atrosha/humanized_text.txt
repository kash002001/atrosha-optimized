Even small outgoing messages can become problematic once combined. Autonomous artificial intelligence systems create challenges for company oversight because they handle money transfers, manage sequences of system interactions, while needing little supervision. A single request leaving the network might seem fine on its own. When grouped, though, these actions bypass limits meant to control expenses, miss required timing rules, wander beyond permitted activities. Monitoring becomes difficult exactly where data exits the organization. A common solution involves using a centralized proxy firewall. Yet this approach tends to break easily. Trust must be given blindly to the proxy, opening risk at one central node while preventing outside verification. Our alternative introduces Proof-Carrying Transactions, or PCT. With PCT, each outgoing agent query becomes its own verifiable object. Instead of relying on intermediaries, these queries embed a zk-SNARK - proof they follow the organization’s stated rules. Rules stay hidden from the user, along with decision boundaries and inner workings of the system. What matters most is how it fits together - this work centers on real-world design, not abstract math. Instead of relying on generic tools, we shaped a special-purpose language for policy limits, then made a translator turning clear outbound conditions into lean mathematical constraints. Efficiency jumps sharply; our method uses just one percent of the computational load seen in broad virtual machines such as RISC Zero. Using an established proving scheme, Groth16 atop BN254 curves, we added custom logic blocks tuned for overseeing artificial intelligence. These include checks based on reduced numerical precision, time-bound validation tags, and layered tree structures tracking approved entries. Testing covered 847 actual corporate guidelines pulled from business settings. Most certificates formed within 95 milliseconds. Timing checks finish in 4.2 milliseconds, always at fixed speed. Most policies - 97.3 percent - translate into fewer than 11,200 R1CS constraints. Sound computation is guaranteed; private data stays hidden. A new condition named policy-binding completeness ensures rule followers pass verification while violators fail every time. Evidence links PCT methods to both the European Union's AI regulations and NIST’s risk framework, proving crypto tools can enforce real-world rules.
